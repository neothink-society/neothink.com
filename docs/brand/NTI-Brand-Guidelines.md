# **2\. Voice & Tone**

## **2.1 The Register**

The Neothink Institute speaks at the register of a serious research institution that also happens to have the most important message in human history. It is not academic (no jargon for its own sake), not populist (no dumbing down), not spiritual (no devotional tone), and not promotional (no hype). It is authoritative, clear, direct, and warm when appropriate.

Think: the tone of a world-class research institution's website crossed with the emotional directness of a movement founder speaking from conviction. Brookings Institution meets Martin Luther King Jr.'s letter from Birmingham Jail.

## **2.2 Core Tone Principles**

**Authoritative without arrogance:** We state what the Unified Field reveals as if it is obvious once you see it. We do not hedge, qualify, or soften the claims. But we never condescend. We speak with the confidence of someone presenting evidence, not the aggression of someone picking a fight.

**Intellectually rigorous:** Every claim connects to the framework. Nothing floats unsupported. When we say 'as force rises, civilization collapses,' we can point to every empire in history. The tone communicates that the proof exists and is available.

**Emotionally direct when it matters:** The Way sections, the personal transformation content, the wound-naming language: these are allowed to be warm, visceral, and direct. 'You are not broken. You were covered.' That is emotional. But even the emotional content is grounded in the structural diagnosis of the Unified Field.

**Never desperate:** We never beg people to pay attention. We never sound urgent in a panicked way. We present the urgency (nuclear annihilation is a mathematical certainty on the current trajectory) with the calm of someone who has done the math and is now building the solution.

**Civilizational scale:** Even when discussing personal transformation, the horizon is always civilizational. The Way is not self-help. It is the individual correction of a 2,400-year civilizational error. Every personal teaching connects to the bigger picture.

## **2.3 What We Never Sound Like**

Never sound like a coaching brand: no 'unlock your potential,' no '10x your results,' no hype language.

Never sound like a spiritual movement: no 'divine light,' no 'energy,' no 'vibrations,' no 'universe wants for you.' We never use the word 'religion' publicly. The Way is a movement or a path, never a religion.

Never sound like a political party: no partisan language, no left/right framing. The Prime Law transcends political categories.

Never sound like AI wrote it: no em dashes. No 'it is important to note that.' No 'it is worth mentioning.' No 'delve into.' No 'in today's world.' No generic filler. Every sentence must carry weight.

Never sound promotional: no 'limited time,' no 'act now,' no urgency language designed to manipulate. The urgency is real (nuclear annihilation). We state it plainly. We do not manufacture it.

## **2.4 Punctuation & Style**

Do not use em dashes. Use commas, periods, or restructure the sentence.

Short paragraphs. Most paragraphs should be 1 to 3 sentences. Occasionally 4 when depth requires it. Never more.

Bold is used sparingly for emphasis on key phrases within body text. Not for decoration.

Italics are used for book titles (Unleashed), for the names of concepts when first introduced, and occasionally for emotional emphasis in Way content.

Oxford comma: yes, always.

Numbers: spell out one through nine, use numerals for 10 and above. Exception: '2,400 years' always uses numerals.

# **4\. Design System**

## **4.1 Color Palette**

The Neothink Institute palette carries **three** gold roles. Each one has a WCAG-tested purpose; using the wrong one on the wrong surface is the single most common contrast mistake in the migration.

**Gold — Display (\#B8973A):** The signature brand gold. Use ONLY as a FILL or LARGE DECORATIVE MARK on light backgrounds, and as TEXT on dark backgrounds. Examples: the logo's "Institute" word, ornamental 1 px rules, 6 px dots, the book-ribbon, the dark hero CTA with ink-colored text, chart-1 accent. Do **not** use as small text or thin UI indicator on a light background — it is only 2.72:1 against paper, which fails WCAG text and UI contrast. Also exposed as the CSS token `--gold` and `--gold-display` (same value).

**Gold — Ink (\#7A6528):** The accessible small-text gold. Use for body-size text, section/eyebrow labels, prose and nav links, borders-as-indicator, focus rings, and button fills where the button has *white* text. Hits 5.98:1 on paper (\#FDFCFA) and 5.43:1 on stone (\#F4F1EC), so it clears AA normal text and 3:1 UI contrast on both cream surfaces. This is the value behind `--gold-ink`, `--gold-ink-deep` (legacy alias), `--gold-muted`, shadcn's `--primary`, and shadcn's `--ring`. When used as a link inside a paragraph, add an underline — gold-vs-body is only ~2.3:1, below the 3:1 link-in-text-block requirement, and the underline is what carries the link affordance.

**Gold — Light (\#D4B060):** Used on dark backgrounds for gold text, emphasis, and italic highlights. Hits 9.36:1 on ink. Not for light backgrounds (fails there). CSS token: `--gold-light`.

**Italic emphasis in headings on light backgrounds** uses Gold — Ink (\#7A6528), not the display gold, for the same contrast reason.

**Ink (\#0E0E0E):** Primary text color on light backgrounds. Dark section backgrounds.

**Stone (\#F4F1EC):** Alternate section background. Light warm gray.

**Stone Mid (\#E8E3D8):** Grid gaps, borders, dividers between cards.

**Stone Dark (\#C8C0B0):** Secondary borders, button outlines.

**Text Primary (\#0E0E0E):** Headlines, bold text, emphasis.

**Text Secondary (\#4A4540):** Body text.

**Text Muted (\#7A7570):** Captions, metadata, footer text.

**White (\#FDFCFA):** Page background. Not pure white. Warm off-white.

## **4.2 Typography**

**Headlines:** Cormorant Garamond, weight 300 (light). Used for all headings, pull quotes, book titles, stage numbers. Gives the institutional, scholarly feel.

**Body Text:** Jost, weight 300 (light). Used for body text, navigation, labels, buttons, metadata. Clean, modern, highly readable.

**Button Text:** Jost, weight 500, 12px, letter-spacing 0.14em, uppercase.

**Section Labels:** Jost, weight 500, 10px, letter-spacing 0.22em, uppercase, Gold — Ink (\#7A6528). Section labels are body-size text, not a logotype, so they use the accessible gold. Display gold (\#B8973A) on this size would fail WCAG.

Font imports (include at top of every HTML widget): Cormorant Garamond (300, 400, 500, 600, italic 300, 400\) and Jost (300, 400, 500, 600).

## **4.3 Layout Patterns**

Max content width: 1160px. Narrow content (long-form reading): 760px.

Section vertical padding: 120px top and bottom on desktop. 80px on mobile.

Horizontal padding: 48px on desktop. 24px on mobile.

Grid gaps: 1px with stone-mid background creates the bordered card look. This is the signature layout pattern.

Dark sections: ink background (\#0E0E0E). Used for statement blocks, Neovia, Prime Law, announcements. Creates visual rhythm alternating with light sections.

Card hover: background shifts from stone to white (on stone backgrounds) or from white to stone (on white backgrounds). Subtle. Never dramatic.

Scroll reveal animation: elements start opacity 0, translateY 20px. Transition to visible on intersection. 0.7s ease. 80ms delay after intersection.

## **4.4 Page Structure Pattern**

Every page follows the same general structure: Hero (title, subtitle, positioning statement), Opening statement (often a dark blockquote section), Main argument or content (long-form narrative in narrow width), Feature grid (cards, capabilities, proof points in max width), Connection section (how this page connects to the rest of the ecosystem with link cards), CTA or closing statement.

Every page connects to every other page through the connection section and through inline references throughout the content.

# **6\. Content Types & Standards**

## **6.1 Website Pages**

Written in the institutional register. Third person for Mark Hamilton's bio and institutional descriptions. Second person ('you') only in Way content and Get Involved content. Every page must connect to the rest of the ecosystem through a connection/links section at the bottom.

## **6.2 Published Work Articles**

These are the deep-dive articles in the Research Library. Each one covers a single topic from the Unified Field, Neovia, Neothink, The Way, or the broader civilizational framework. They should read like long-form essays from a serious intellectual journal. 1,500 to 3,000 words. Authoritative, well-structured, historically grounded. Draw directly from the Unleashed manuscript for source material.

## **6.3 Podcast Episodes**

Twelve founding episodes covering the complete arc of the Unified Field. Mark delivers the civilizational architecture. Wallace translates it to the personal/visceral. Father-son dynamic is the drama. Real conversations first, build episodes around the moments that surprise both.

## **6.4 Shorts**

30 to 90 seconds. 3 to 5 per week. Way shorts (wound-naming, caged mind, two worlds) and UF/Neovia shorts (2,400-year detour, nuclear certainty, Prime Law). Wallace's voice. Direct to camera. Short paragraphs, ellipses for dramatic pauses, standalone two-word sentences, no logical connectors, curiosity-deepening rather than curiosity-satisfying.

## **6.5 Email Communications**

Warm, accountable, willing to go beyond stated policy when the relationship warrants it. For The Society and community: the 7-email warmup 'Orientation' sequence. For prospects: the Greatest Kept Secret of All Time booklet as entry point, flowing through invitation letter, masterclass, and into membership.

## **6.6 The Way Teachings (Internal: 'Sermons')**

Wallace solo. Proclamation arc, not curriculum. Confessional, rhythmic, percussion-driven delivery style. Movement 1 (1-4): Diagnosis (caged mind, covering, false world). Movement 2 (5-8): Return (ground, truth, sight, clearing). Movement 3 (9-12): Life (living, golden world, transmission, horizon).
